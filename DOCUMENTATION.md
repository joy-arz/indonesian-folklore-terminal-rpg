## Overview

**AI Terminal RPG** is an educational terminal-based game that teaches Indonesian history and culture through interactive storytelling. Using Cerebras AI's Llama model, the game creates immersive narratives set in historical Indonesian kingdoms.

**Educational Focus:** Indonesian history, culture, and folklore (7th-17th century)
**Language:** English (with authentic Indonesian terms)
**Platform:** Windows, macOS, Linux
**Python Version:** 3.8+

---

## 🎓 Educational Mission

### Learning Objectives

This game is designed to teach players about:

1. **Historical Kingdoms** - Accurate timelines, locations, and key figures
2. **Cultural Diversity** - Regional differences across the Indonesian archipelago
3. **Traditional Architecture** - Candi, pendopo, joglo, and palace structures
4. **Social Hierarchy** - Kings, priests, warriors, merchants, and commoners
5. **Trade & Economy** - Spice trade, maritime routes, and cultural exchange
6. **Religion & Philosophy** - Hindu-Buddhist syncretism and Islamic synthesis
7. **Mythology & Folklore** - Traditional creatures and spiritual beliefs
8. **Language & Terms** - Authentic Indonesian vocabulary in context

### Pedagogical Approach

- **Seamless Integration** - Historical facts woven naturally into narrative
- **Active Learning** - Players make choices that teach consequences
- **Cultural Immersion** - Experience daily life in different kingdoms
- **Authentic Representation** - Accurate depiction of clothing, food, customs
- **Contextual Learning** - Indonesian terms explained through usage

## 🏛️ Indonesian Kingdoms - Historical Guide

### 1. Sriwijaya Empire (7th-14th Century)

**Location:** Palembang, South Sumatra
**Religion:** Mahayana Buddhism
**Language:** Old Malay with Sanskrit influences

#### Historical Significance
- Controlled the strategic Strait of Malacca trade route
- Major center of Buddhist learning attracting scholars from China and India
- First unified maritime empire in Southeast Asia
- Mentioned in Chinese Tang Dynasty records (671 CE)

#### Cultural Features
- **Architecture:** Multi-tiered pagodas, stone inscriptions (prasasti)
- **Trade:** Spices, gold, ivory, pearls, and exotic woods
- **Education:** Nalanda University connection, Buddhist manuscripts
- **Government:** Mandala system with tributary states

#### Key Historical Facts
- Kedukan Bukit Inscription (683 CE) - oldest Old Malay inscription
- Talang Tuwo Inscription - describes Buddhist garden dedication
- Chinese monk Yijing studied Sanskrit in Sriwijaya for 6 years
- Declined due to Chola Empire raids (1025 CE)

#### In-Game Experience
You'll explore Palembang's bustling port, witness Buddhist ceremonies, and protect trade routes from rival kingdoms.

---

### 2. Majapahit Empire (1293-1527)

**Location:** Trowulan, East Java
**Religion:** Hindu-Buddhist syncretism (Siwa-Buddha)
**Language:** Old Javanese (Kawi)

#### Historical Significance
- Largest empire in Indonesian history
- United most of modern-day Indonesia under one flag
- Golden age of art, literature, and architecture
- Famous for the Nagarakretagama epic poem

#### Key Historical Figures
- **Raden Wijaya** (1293-1309) - Founder, escaped Mongol invasion
- **Tribhuwana Wijayatunggadewi** (1328-1350) - Queen who expanded the empire
- **Gajah Mada** (Prime Minister 1331-1364) - Architect of unity
- **Prabu Hayam Wuruk** (1350-1389) - Peak of Majapahit power
- **Prabu Siliwangi** - Allied king of Pajajaran

#### The Sumpah Palapa Oath
Gajah Mada's famous oath to unite Nusantara (Indonesian archipelago):
> "I will not taste spices (palapa) until I have conquered all the islands under the sky."

This oath drove Majapahit's expansion across modern Indonesia, Singapore, Malaysia, and the Philippines.

#### Cultural Features
- **Architecture:** Red brick temples (candi), pendopo pavilions
- **Arts:** Wayang kulit (shadow puppets), gamelan orchestras
- **Literature:** Kakawin poetry, Pararaton chronicle
- **Agriculture:** Advanced irrigation (subak), rice terraces
- **Social Structure:** Four castes (Brahmana, Ksatria, Waisya, Sudra)

#### In-Game Experience
Start in Trowulan's alun-alun (royal square), witness Gajah Mada's oath, and participate in campaigns to unite the archipelago.

### 3. Pajajaran / Sunda Kingdom (932-1579)

**Location:** Pakuan (modern Bogor), West Java
**People:** Sundanese
**Religion:** Hindu (Sunda Wiwitan)

#### Historical Significance
- Last Hindu kingdom in West Java
- Known for agricultural prosperity and rice cultivation
- Famous for the legendary King Prabu Siliwangi
- Fell to Islamic Demak Sultanate in 1579

#### Key Historical Figures
- **Prabu Siliwangi** (1482-1521) - Legendary king, considered a deity
- Known for justice, wisdom, and supernatural powers
- Symbol of Sundanese cultural identity

#### Cultural Features
- **Agriculture:** Sacred rice cultivation traditions
- **Pusaka:** Sacred heirlooms with spiritual power (Keris Ciung Wanara)
- **Architecture:** Wooden palaces, fortified compounds
- **Martial Arts:** Pencak Silat traditions
- **Textiles:** Batik with unique Sundanese patterns

#### The Mystery of Prabu Siliwangi
Legend says Prabu Siliwangi didn't die but transformed into a tiger (harimau) and disappeared into the forest with his loyal followers.

#### In-Game Experience
Defend the kingdom's sacred pusaka, experience Sundanese rice farming traditions, and face the Islamic expansion from the north coast.

---

### 4. Demak Sultanate (1475-1548)

**Location:** Demak, North Coast Java
**Religion:** Islam (Sunni)
**Language:** Javanese with Arabic influences

#### Historical Significance
- First Islamic Sultanate in Java
- Center of Islamic propagation (dakwah)
- Led by the Wali Songo (Nine Saints)
- Marked the transition from Hindu-Buddhist to Islamic era

#### Key Historical Figures
- **Raden Patah** (1475-1518) - First Sultan, son of Majapahit king
- **Sunan Kalijaga** - Famous wali, used wayang for Islamic teaching
- **Sunan Kudus** - Tolerant scholar, forbade cow sacrifice to respect Hindus
- **Sultan Trenggana** (1518-1546) - Expanded Demak's power

#### Cultural Synthesis
- **Architecture:** Demak Great Mosque with tatal (wood chip) roof
- **Arts:** Wayang kulit adapted for Islamic stories
- **Education:** Pesantren (Islamic boarding schools)
- **Philosophy:** Javanese-Islamic mystical synthesis (Kejawen)

#### The Wali Songo (Nine Saints)
Nine Islamic scholars who spread Islam through cultural adaptation:
1. Sunan Gresik - Trade and commerce
2. Sunan Ampel - Education in Surabaya
3. Sunan Bonang - Music and gamelan
4. Sunan Giri - Islamic boarding school
5. Sunan Drajat - Social welfare
6. Sunan Kalijaga - Wayang and arts
7. Sunan Kudus - Interfaith tolerance
8. Sunan Muria - Mountain communities
9. Sunan Gunung Jati - West Java expansion

#### In-Game Experience
Learn Islamic-Javanese synthesis, participate in dakwah through arts, and navigate the transition from Hindu to Islamic rule.

---

### 5. Mataram Islam (1587-1755)

**Location:** Kotagede, then Plered (Central Java)
**Religion:** Islam with Javanese traditions
**Language:** Javanese

#### Historical Significance
- Unified Central Java after Demak's decline
- Developed distinctive Javanese-Islamic court culture
- Fought against Dutch VOC (East India Company)
- Split into Surakarta and Yogyakarta (1755)

#### Key Historical Figures
- **Panembahan Senopati** (1587-1601) - Founder, mystical warrior-king
- **Sultan Agung** (1613-1645) - Greatest sultan, attacked Dutch Batavia
- **Amangkurat I** (1645-1677) - Controversial ruler, Trunajaya rebellion
- **Trunajaya** - Rebel prince from Madura

#### Cultural Features
- **Architecture:** Kotagede palace, Plered ruins
- **Calendar:** Javanese calendar (Saka) reformed by Sultan Agung
- **Arts:** Court gamelan, bedhaya dance, wayang wong
- **Literature:** Babad (chronicles), serat (moral teachings)
- **Religion:** Kejawen (Javanese mysticism)

#### Sultan Agung's Legacy
- United most of Java except Banten and Batavia (Dutch)
- Created the Javanese calendar combining Saka and Islamic systems
- Wrote literary works and composed gamelan pieces
- Led two attacks on Dutch Batavia (1628, 1629)

#### In-Game Experience
Navigate court intrigue, face the Dutch VOC threat, and experience the height of Javanese-Islamic civilization.

---

### 6. Tanjung Pura Kingdom (14th-17th Century)

**Location:** West Kalimantan (Borneo) coast
**Culture:** Malay-Dayak synthesis
**Trade:** Diamonds, gold, forest products

#### Historical Significance
- Mysterious kingdom that disappeared overnight
- Represents the Malay-Dayak cultural fusion
- Important diamond and gold trading post
- Subject of local legends and mysteries

#### The Mystery
Historical records and local legends tell of an entire kingdom that vanished in a single night. Archaeological evidence suggests a prosperous trading port that was suddenly abandoned around 1700 CE.

#### Cultural Features
- **Trade:** Diamonds from Meratus Mountains, gold, exotic woods
- **Culture:** Blend of Malay Islamic and Dayak animist traditions
- **Architecture:** Stilt houses, river-based settlements
- **Crafts:** Diamond cutting, gold smithing, beadwork

#### In-Game Experience
Investigate the mysterious disappearance, explore diamond mines, and uncover the truth behind one of Indonesia's greatest historical mysteries.

## 🌏 Cultural Regions & Traditions

### East Java (Majapahit Region)

**Cultural Identity:** Javanese Hindu-Buddhist
**Key Features:**
- Red brick architecture (candi)
- Wayang kulit shadow puppetry
- Gamelan orchestras
- Reog Ponorogo dance
- Culinary: Rawon, Rujak Cingur

**Historical Sites:**
- Trowulan ruins (Majapahit capital)
- Penataran temple complex
- Tikus bathing place

---

### West Java (Sunda/Pajajaran Region)

**Cultural Identity:** Sundanese
**Key Features:**
- Angklung bamboo music
- Jaipongan traditional dance
- Wayang golek (wooden puppets)
- Pencak Silat martial arts
- Culinary: Nasi Timbel, Sate Maranggi

**Historical Sites:**
- Batu Tulis inscriptions (Bogor)
- Pakuan palace ruins
- Cangkuang temple

---

### South Sumatra (Sriwijaya Region)

**Cultural Identity:** Malay-Buddhist
**Key Features:**
- Songket weaving
- Zapin dance
- Traditional boat making
- Culinary: Pempek, Pindang

**Historical Sites:**
- Kedukan Bukit inscription
- Muara Takus temple
- Srivijaya Archaeological Park (Palembang)

---

### North Coast Java (Demak Region)

**Cultural Identity:** Javanese-Islamic (Pasisir culture)
**Key Features:**
- Islamic architecture with Javanese elements
- Gambus music
- Seblang ritual dance
- Culinary: Soto Kudus, Nasi Bogana

**Historical Sites:**
- Demak Great Mosque
- Menara Kudus (Kudus Tower)
- Sendang Duwur mosque

---

### Central Java (Mataram Region)

**Cultural Identity:** Javanese-Islamic Court Culture
**Key Features:**
- Keraton (palace) traditions
- Bedhaya court dance
- Gamelan sekaten
- Batik with court patterns
- Culinary: Gudeg, Nasi Liwet

**Historical Sites:**
- Kotagede (old Mataram capital)
- Plered ruins
- Imogiri royal cemetery

---

### Kalimantan (Tanjung Pura Region)

**Cultural Identity:** Malay-Dayak Synthesis
**Key Features:**
- Longhouse architecture
- Mandau (traditional sword)
- Hudoq mask dance
- Diamond and gold crafts
- Culinary: Soto Banjar, Ketupat Kandangan

**Historical Sites:**
- Tanjung Pura ruins
- Diamond mines (Martapura)
- Traditional river settlements

## Gameplay Guide

### Educational Game Flow

```
1. Starting Point Selection (1 of 10 historical scenarios)
   ↓
2. Historical Context Introduction (year, kingdom, location)
   ↓
3. Story Scene with Cultural Details + 3 Choices
   ↓
4. Player Makes Choice (learning consequences)
   ↓
5. AI Generates Next Scene (with historical facts)
   ↓
6. Encounter Check (combat with mythological creatures)
   ↓
7. Combat or Shop (using Indonesian items)
   ↓
8. Repeat until turn 500-700 or death
   ↓
9. Ending (based on player actions and historical impact)
```

## Game Systems

## Troubleshooting

### "trpg: command not found"

**Solution:**
```bash
# Find Python scripts path
python -c "import sysconfig; print(sysconfig.get_path('scripts'))"

# Add to PATH (macOS/Linux)
echo 'export PATH="$(python3 -m site --user-base)/bin:$PATH"' >> ~/.bashrc
source ~/.bashrc

# Or run directly
python -m trpg
```

### "No API key found"

**Solution:**
1. Get API key from https://cloud.cerebras.ai/
2. Create `.env` file in game directory
3. Add: `CEREBRAS_API_KEY=your_key_here`

### "Module not found"

**Solution:**
```bash
pip install openai python-dotenv colorama
```
